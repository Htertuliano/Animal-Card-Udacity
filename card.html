Artic Fox (Vulpes lagopus)

The Artic Fox, though lovely and gorgeous, has been shrouded in confusion as to the proper naming convention due to its similarity with the finnec fox and their bahaviorial compatibility.

  1. Habitat: Lives in the tundra and coat changes color with the seasons
  2. Prey: Usually hunts small rodents such as voles, hares and lemmings.
  3. Predators: Polar bears, Wolf packs and Humans are the three threats to this fierce predator
  4. Mates: These are monogamous creatures and only take one mate for life.

The artic fox holds a warm spot in my heart for its beautiful coat and eyes. It is a fierce predator and cannot be outwitted. It has always been a favorite of mine.
